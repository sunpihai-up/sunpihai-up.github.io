## About Me

I am a M.S. student at **Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT)**. My research lies in
**computer vision** and **robot perception**, with a focus on **monocular depth estimation**,
**event-based vision**, and **multimodal (image–event) fusion** for robust perception under
challenging conditions. More recently, I have also been working on perception for
**embodied AI and humanoid robots**.

I am advised by [Prof. Junjun Jiang](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=WNH2_rgAAAAJ&hl=en)
at HIT. I am currently a research intern at **Beijing Humanoid Robot Innovation Center
(X-Humanoid)**, where I work under the guidance of
[Qiang (Jony) Zhang](https://github.com/jonyzhang2023).

## Research Interests

My research is dedicated to building robust 3D perception systems that can recover reliable
geometric and semantic understanding from imperfect multimodal observations, including images,
events, point clouds, and degraded visual inputs. I aim to make perception reliable under
challenging real-world conditions such as low light, motion blur, specular and transparent
surfaces, sparse sensing, and domain shifts.

For embodied AI, I am particularly interested in connecting perception with action: enabling
humanoid robots to perceive traversable structure, reason about complex terrain, build multimodal
occupancy representations, and use these signals for closed-loop locomotion and real-world autonomy.

## News

- **[2026]** We released **Omni**, a breakthrough humanoid robot platform for robust perceptive locomotion. I developed its perceptive-locomotion capability, enabling 15-minute stable walking in randomized urban scenes with lawns, slopes, diverse stairs, and rough ground; one-shot ascent of 17 indoor fire-stair floors; and robust traversal of compound terrain with stairs, stepping-stone piles, gaps, and moving platforms.
