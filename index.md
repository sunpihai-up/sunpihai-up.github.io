## About Me

From 2019 to 2023, I studied at the **College of Computer Science and Technology,
Qingdao University**, where I received my B.Eng. degree. Since 2023, I have been pursuing
an M.S. degree in **Electronic Information (Computer Technology)** at the **Faculty of
Computing, Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT)**, advised by
[Prof. Junjun Jiang](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=WNH2_rgAAAAJ&hl=en).
I will graduate in **June 2026**.

My research is centered on **robust 3D perception** and **embodied AI**. I work on reliable
geometric and semantic understanding from multimodal or degraded observations, and on deploying
perception algorithms for humanoid robots. I am currently a research intern at **Beijing
Humanoid Robot Innovation Center (X-Humanoid)**, where I work under the guidance of
[Qiang (Jony) Zhang](https://github.com/jonyzhang2023) on perception and perceptive locomotion.

## Research Interests

My research is dedicated to building robust 3D perception systems that can recover reliable
geometric and semantic understanding from imperfect multimodal observations, including images,
events, point clouds, and degraded visual inputs. I aim to make perception reliable under
challenging real-world conditions such as low light, motion blur, specular and transparent
surfaces, sparse sensing, and domain shifts.

For embodied AI, I am particularly interested in connecting perception with action: enabling
humanoid robots to perceive traversable structure, reason about complex terrain, build multimodal
occupancy representations, and use these signals for closed-loop locomotion and real-world autonomy.
